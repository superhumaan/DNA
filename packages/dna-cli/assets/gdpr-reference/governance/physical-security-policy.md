Physical Security Policy

## Uk Gdpr

Purpose

Addresses physical security for [Company Name] premises, personnel devices, and reliance on Microsoft Azure datacentre controls for [Product Name] production.

Complements logical controls for defence in depth.

### The objective of this document is to ensure that

protect personnel, devices, and paper records from unauthorised physical access,

document reliance on Azure physical controls for production data,

ensure secure disposal of hardware.

### This document establishes

office access and visitor controls

Azure datacentre assurance

### This document supports

Remote Working Policy

Scope

This document governs [Company Name] offices, co-working, home offices as referenced in Remote Working Policy, and personnel devices for [Company Name] in relation to the [Product Name] platform.

Territorial scope: United Kingdom (England, Wales, Scotland, and Northern Ireland) only.

Platform scope: multi-tenant SaaS hosted in Microsoft Azure UK South; excludes non-production sandboxes unless explicitly stated.

Azure UK South physical security is vendor-controlled per Microsoft attestations

Regulatory Alignment

This document implements [Company Name] accountability under UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.

Compliance MUST be demonstrated with operational records assignable to named owners — not policy text alone.

Accountability under Article 5(2) and Article 24 UK GDPR.

Control Objectives

Protect personnel, devices, and paper records from unauthorised physical access.

Document reliance on Azure physical controls for production data.

Ensure secure disposal of hardware.

Governance Structure

Governance authority resides with [Company Name] Executive leadership, the Data Protection Officer, Information Security, and Engineering.

Material exceptions and residual risks require Executive or DPO approval as specified in this document.

Office and Visitors

Mandatory controls

Office access MUST use badge control where [Company Name] maintains dedicated premises.

Visitors MUST sign in, receive badge, and be escorted in areas where Confidential information visible.

Tailgating MUST be challenged; personnel MUST NOT hold secure doors open for unknown individuals.

Delivery of assets MUST be logged; unexpected packages investigated before opening in secure areas.

Fire and environmental procedures MUST be documented with annual drill where premises occupied.

Azure and Production Data

Mandatory controls

[Product Name] production data processing occurs in Azure UK South; physical datacentre security relies on Microsoft ISO/SOC reports reviewed annually.

Azure physical compliance reports MUST be obtained annually and reviewed by Security.

No production customer data on removable media except encrypted Security-approved cases.

Workstations and Media

Mandatory controls

Workstations MUST be locked when unattended; cable locks required in shared offices.

Paper records containing personal data MUST be stored locked and shredded cross-cut when disposed.

Printers MUST not retain Confidential jobs; secure print release where available.

Server rooms on-premises, if any, MUST have separate access control and environmental monitoring.

Disposal and Incidents

Mandatory controls

Hardware disposal MUST use certified IT asset disposal with destruction certificate for devices that held Restricted data.

Physical breach (theft, break-in) MUST be reported to Security within one hour.

Home offices MUST follow Remote Working Policy for device and screen controls.

Operational Procedure

The following procedure SHALL be executed for every in-scope event. Deviations require DPO approval.

1. Facilities maintains access badge list synced with HR monthly.

2. Visitors logged at reception; escort assigned.

3. Annual office security walkthrough checklist completed by Facilities and Security.

4. IT schedules certified disposal pickups with certificate filing.

5. Security reviews Microsoft datacentre compliance reports annually; gaps escalated to Vendor Manager.

6. Physical incident: secure area, notify Security and police if theft; assess data on stolen devices.

7. Update risk register with physical findings.

8. Communicate reminders on clear desk quarterly.

9. Badge list reconciled with HR monthly.

10. Visitor log sampled monthly by Facilities.

11. Azure SOC/ISO review notes filed annually.

12. Physical incident linked to Incident Response when devices at risk.
