LIA Requirements

Mandatory controls

Processing on legitimate interests MUST NOT begin until LIA is approved and linked from ROPA.

Each LIA SHALL document purpose, necessity, balancing, safeguards, opt-out mechanism where applicable, and review date.

Direct marketing MUST have separate PECR compliance check recorded in LIA annex.

Negative balancing outcome MUST block processing until alternative lawful basis is documented.

LIA register MUST be accessible to auditors with version history.

Prohibited and Sensitive Processing

Mandatory controls

Children or vulnerable individuals processing MUST NOT use legitimate interests without DPO and Legal written rejection of lower bases.

Large-scale profiling for decisions with legal or similar effect is prohibited; LIA cannot authorise Article 22 processing.

Legitimate interests for security logging MUST limit content to metadata; workspace body text MUST NOT be logged for security analytics.

Product analytics MUST use pseudonymised identifiers and aggregated reporting; individual workspace content analysis is prohibited.

Objections and Review

Mandatory controls

Objections under Article 21 MUST be assessed within 30 days; LIA MUST be revisited if upheld.

LIAs MUST be reviewed annually and upon material scope change (new data source, new recipient, new region).

Third-party sharing for LIA purposes MUST be listed in ROPA with contract reference.

Delegated marketing LIAs require DPO countersignature.

Emergency and Interim Processing

Mandatory controls

Emergency fraud prevention MAY proceed with interim LIA completion within 10 working days.

Interim LIAs MUST be flagged in register with hard expiry and DPO review date.

Retired processing triggers LIA archival with end date recorded.

Operational Procedure

The following procedure SHALL be executed for every in-scope event. Deviations require DPO approval.

1. Product submits LIA intake with purpose, data categories, recipients, retention, and alternatives rejected with reasons.

2. Engineering confirms whether processing is strictly necessary and describes minimisation controls.

3. DPO completes balancing test documenting impacts, mitigations, and opt-out or transparency measures.

4. Legal reviews PECR position for electronic marketing uses.

5. Approved LIA assigned reference ID and linked in ROPA within two working days.

6. DPO schedules annual review reminder; Product notified 30 days before review due.

7. Article 21 objection intake triggers LIA reassessment and temporary suspension if required.

8. Retired processing triggers LIA archival with end date recorded.

9. Quarterly sample of three LIAs reviewed for continued validity.

10. PECR annex updated when marketing channels or content change materially.
